## 2. Variant Concepts (test all 5, double down on the winner)

### Variant A — Anatomy Reveal
Slow-motion technique → pause → x-ray reveals firing muscles → resume.
- Hook: *"Watch the cross from the inside."*
- Audience: education-curious, biomechanics nerds, S&C coaches.

### Variant B — Beginner Magnet
Person fumbling a basic jab → opens Champion → 30 seconds later their jab is sharp.
- Hook: *"Day 1 vs. Champion app on Day 1."*
- Audience: zero-to-hero positioning, MMA-curious lurkers.

### Variant C — Coach Reaction
Real coach watching the filter, narrating: *"This is exactly what I tell my fighters."*
- Hook: *"A coach reacts to Champion."*
- Audience: credibility play, gym-network seeding.

### Variant D — The Comparison
Wrestle AI requires upload + costs $15/mo. Champion is free. Side-by-side.
- Hook: *"I cancelled my $17/month MMA app for this free one."*
- Audience: cost-conscious users, switchers.

### Variant E — The Gap
Pro fighter executes → your form executes → filter on both → see the gap.
- Hook: *"This is why your kicks have no power."*
- Audience: intermediate fighters who plateaued.

## 7. First Gym Validation (Before Public Launch)

Bring the app to your home gym. Show three fighters at different levels:

1. **Beginner** (3 months training) — does the filter clarify what their coach has been telling them?
2. **Intermediate** (1–2 years) — does seeing the muscle map reveal a leak they didn't know they had?
3. **Advanced** (5+ years) — does it match their actual kinesthetic experience?

Ask each person:
- "Does this match what you actually feel during the technique?"
- "Would you use this between rounds?"
- "Would you pay $6/month for the upload version?"

**Capture the reactions on video** — that footage *becomes* Variant C (Coach Reaction) content. You're filming your launch ad while doing user research.

## 9. The Single Metric That Matters

Forget vanity. The only first-30-days metric that matters:

> **Day-3 retention.**
